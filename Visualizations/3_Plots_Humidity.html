Humidity

Humidity appears to be inversely related to distance from the Equator, depending on which hemisphere is being observed. In the Southern Hemisphere, humidity tends to increase with proximity to the equator, while in the Northern Hemisphere humidity tends to decrease with greater distance from the equator.

This trend can be observed in the scatter plot showing the spread of City Latitude vs. Humidity for the Northern Hemisphere and Southern Hemisphere. However, for hemispheres, it is important to note that the data points have a wide spread, so the trend does not have a strong correlation.

Lower humidity at higher latitudes in the Northern Hemisphere may be due to the data being taken during the winter, when climates are generally drier.
